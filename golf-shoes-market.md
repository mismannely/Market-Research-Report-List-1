<p><p>The golf shoes market is primarily divided into two types: spiked and spikeless. Spiked golf shoes feature protruding studs on the outsole, providing enhanced traction and stability on the course, ideal for wet or uneven surfaces. They are often favored by traditionalists seeking maximum grip. Conversely, spikeless golf shoes have a flat sole with specialized tread patterns, offering versatility for on-course and casual wear. They prioritize comfort and convenience, appealing to golfers who value ease of movement and style.</p></p>
