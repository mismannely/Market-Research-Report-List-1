<p><p>The liver cancer drugs market comprises various treatment types, primarily targeted therapy and immunotherapy. Targeted therapy involves using drugs that specifically attack cancer cells while sparing healthy tissue, often focusing on genetic mutations and pathways involved in tumor growth. Immunotherapy harnesses the body's immune system to recognize and destroy cancer cells, enhancing immune responses through checkpoint inhibitors or cell-based therapies. Both approaches aim to improve treatment outcomes and patient survival while minimizing side effects associated with traditional chemotherapy.</p></p>
